Two Industries, One Skillset

NDIS and aged care are closer than you think

👥

Same People, Different Stage of Life

Your NDIS participants have parents and grandparents who need aged care. Many aged care clients previously had disability support. You already understand the people — it's the same community, at a different life stage.

🔄

Same Core Work, Less Complexity

Both roles coordinate care, manage budgets, liaise with providers, and advocate for clients. Aged care is a connection service — no behaviour support plans, no restrictive practices, no SIL. Andrew Wallace: “Nothing in aged care is harder than NDIS.”

🌐

Same Provider Network

Physios, OTs, nurses, personal carers, cleaners — the allied health and support worker network you already use operates across both NDIS and aged care. Your existing relationships transfer directly.

🏢

Same Government Funding Structure

Both are government-funded individual budgets managed by a registered provider. You already understand plan budgets, service bookings, compliance, and claiming. The system works the same way — just different legislation.
