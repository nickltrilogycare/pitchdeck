Talking to Potential Clients (65+ or Their Families)

The opening question

Use this in any context — whether at a community event, in a GP waiting room, or chatting with someone at a retirement village:

"Are you getting the most from your Support at Home funding?"

Your single most powerful opening line

This question works because:

  • It assumes they already have funding (validates them)
  • It implies there's a "more" to get (creates curiosity)
  • It's not salesy — it's genuinely helpful

Key questions to ask

Once the conversation is open, use these questions to understand their situation:

  1. "How are you finding your current home care arrangement?"
  2. "Do you feel like you're getting enough care hours from your budget?"
  3. "Are you able to choose your own care workers?"
  4. "How much of your budget goes to management fees vs actual care?"
  5. "Have you heard about the new Support at Home program?"

Explain the model in plain English

Copy & paste
Your Elevator Pitch

"I work as a care coordinator with Trilogy Care, one of Australia's largest Support at Home providers. My job is to help you get the most from your government home care funding — I coordinate all your services, find good local workers, and make sure your care plan works for you. Trilogy handles all the admin, billing, and compliance behind the scenes. The result? More of your budget goes to actual care, less goes to fees."

Common Client Objections & Responses

They Say You Say
"I'm happy with my current provider" "That's great — can I ask, do you feel you're getting the most care hours from your budget? Some of our clients found they got 15–20% more care after switching."
"I don't want to change" "Totally understand. Switching is actually very simple — just 3 steps, and your funding transfers automatically. But there's no pressure at all. Here's my card if you ever want to explore it."
"My family handles everything" "That's wonderful that you have family support. We actually work WITH families — our role is to take the coordination burden off them so they can focus on just being family."
"I don't understand how it works" "It's simpler than it sounds. The government gives you a budget for home care. We help you spend that budget on the services you actually need — cleaning, nursing, physio, whatever it is. We do the organising, you enjoy the care."

When to involve Trilogy

Handoff Points

Client is ready to proceed: Collect their name, phone number, and My Aged Care reference number. Call Trilogy on 1300 459 190 or submit a referral form.

They want more info: Leave a brochure and follow up in one week.

Talking to Referral Sources (GPs, Hospitals, Allied Health)

Hospital discharge planner

Script — Hospital Discharge

"Hi, I'm [Name], a Support at Home care coordinator working with Trilogy Care. I help patients transitioning home get the care services they need — we coordinate everything from personal care to nursing to allied health. Can I leave some information for when you have patients being discharged who need home care support?"

GP practice manager

Script — GP Practice

"I'm [Name], I coordinate home care services for people over 65 in the area, through Trilogy Care. If any of your patients are looking for help managing their Support at Home funding, I'd love to be a referral option. Can I leave some brochures?"

Allied health professional

Script — Allied Health

"Hi [Name], I know you see a lot of elderly patients. I've recently started offering aged care coordination through Trilogy Care — I can help your patients access the right home care services under their Support at Home budget. Would you be open to cross-referring?"

Leave-behind materials

Always bring your co-branded brochure. Leave 5–10 at each location. Replenish on follow-up visits.

Follow-up cadence: Visit monthly for hospitals, quarterly for GPs and allied health.

Talking to NDIS Clients About Their Parents

This is your warmest lead source. You already have a relationship. Here's how to make the conversation natural:

Copy & paste
The Natural Bridge Script

"By the way, [Client Name], I've started doing something new — I'm now also coordinating aged care services for older people through Trilogy Care. If your mum or dad is over 65 and gets help at home, or might need it, I might be able to help them get more from their government funding. Would that be worth a chat?"

Why this works

  • It's a natural extension of your existing relationship
  • You're offering help, not selling
  • Parents of NDIS participants are often in the aged care demographic
  • They already trust you

From Conversation to Client

When someone says "yes, I'm interested" — here's exactly what happens next:

  1. Collect information: Full name, phone number, My Aged Care reference number (if they have one)
  2. Ask: "Do you currently have a Support at Home provider?" (If yes, the switching process applies)
  3. Submit referral to Trilogy via the partner portal or by calling your Partnership Manager
  4. Trilogy contacts the client within 2 business days to begin onboarding
  5. You're introduced as their dedicated coordinator
  6. Timeline: Conversation to active client = 2–4 weeks

Quick Reference

Trilogy Referral Line: 1300 459 190

Referral Info Needed: Client name + phone + My Aged Care reference number

Client Contact SLA: Within 2 business days of referral
