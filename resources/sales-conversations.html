Aged Care Conversations for NDIS Coordinators

12 min read

You Already Know How to Do This

NDIS coordination requires navigating plan budgets, complex family dynamics, multiple funding bodies, compliance obligations, and emotionally charged conversations about disability and dependence. Aged care conversations are simpler — and the skills transfer directly.

"If you can navigate an NDIS plan review, you can have an aged care conversation."

The language is different. The skills are the same.

The key is translation. This guide gives you the language to move between NDIS and aged care framing so conversations feel natural — to you and to the people you're speaking with.

The NDIS-to-Aged-Care Language Map

Use the right language for each context. Neither is better — they're just different dialects of the same profession.

Talking to Older Clients (65+)

The opening question

Use this in any context — a family visit, a GP waiting room, or a conversation at your NDIS participant's home when their parent is present:

"Is your mum or dad getting any help at home from the government?"

Simple, warm, and opens the door without any jargon

Follow with:

  • "How are they finding the support they're getting?"
  • "Do you feel they're getting enough care hours from what they're entitled to?"
  • "Have they heard about the new Support at Home programme?"

Explain the model in plain English

Your Elevator Pitch

"I coordinate aged care services for older people through Future Focus — working alongside Trilogy Care, one of Australia's largest home care providers. My role is to help your family member get the most from their government funding — things like cleaning, personal care, nursing, physio, whatever they need to stay at home safely. Trilogy handles all the billing and admin behind the scenes. I just coordinate."

Talking to Families

Families of older clients are often the decision-makers — just like parents of NDIS participants. Your experience here is directly transferable.

Copy & paste
Script for Adult Children

"I know you're already juggling a lot looking after your mum or dad. The good news is there's government funding available that most families don't realise they're entitled to. I can help you work out what they qualify for, set up all the services, and manage it on an ongoing basis. You don't have to do any of the coordination yourself — that's what I do."

Why families respond to Future Focus

  • They already know NDIS is complex — you're positioned as the person who handles complexity
  • If you already coordinate for another family member, you're a known, trusted person
  • The "whole family" framing resonates — one coordinator for the whole family's care needs

Talking to Hospital Discharge Planners

Script — Hospital Discharge

"Hi, I'm [Name] from Future Focus. We coordinate NDIS supports across [location], and we've recently extended into aged care through a partnership with Trilogy Care. If you're discharging patients who need home care organised quickly, I'd love to be a referral option — I can move fast and I understand how to navigate government funding systems. Can I leave some details?"

Your NDIS advantage

Discharge planners deal with providers who are slow and process-heavy. Your NDIS background means you know how to move quickly, navigate bureaucracy, and communicate clearly with families under stress. Lead with this.

Talking to GPs

Script — GP Practice

"Hi, I'm [Name] from Future Focus. We do NDIS coordination in the area, and we've now added aged care through Trilogy Care. If any of your older patients are struggling at home or their families are looking for support, I can help them access government-funded home care. I handle all the coordination — My Aged Care, assessments, care planning, services. Can I leave some brochures?"

Objection Handling

They Say You Say
"We already have a provider" "That's great. Can I ask — are they getting the most from their budget? Some of our clients found they got significantly more care hours after switching, because fees were lower. Happy to have a no-pressure chat about it."
"Aged care sounds complicated" "Honestly, compared to NDIS it's quite manageable. We've navigated complex disability plans for years — aged care is a natural extension of that skillset. That's exactly why we partnered with Trilogy Care to do it properly."
"They don't want to change anything" "Totally understandable. Switching is actually very simple — Trilogy handles the paperwork, funding transfers automatically, and care can continue without interruption. No pressure at all. Here's a brochure if they ever want to explore it."
"I didn't know you did aged care" "It's new — we've just launched this through Future Focus, in partnership with Trilogy Care. Same team, same coordination approach, but for Support at Home rather than NDIS. We thought it made sense given how many families we already support."
"What's the fee structure?" "Trilogy Care charges a coordination fee from the client's government funding — typically 10–15%. That's lower than most traditional providers who charge 25–35%. The difference goes directly to care services."

The Natural Bridge: NDIS Clients and Their Parents

This is your warmest lead source. You already have the relationship and the trust. The conversation is a natural extension of care you're already providing to the family.
