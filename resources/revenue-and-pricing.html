Revenue & Fee Structure

12 min read — Enterprise fee model for Future Focus

The Enterprise Fee Model

Future Focus earns a coordination fee of 10–15% on the services coordinated for each aged care client. This percentage is agreed in the enterprise price book and applies across all 9 locations. The fee is calculated on the value of services your coordinators arrange — not on billable hours.

How revenue flows

  1. Each client has a Support at Home budget (e.g., $29,696/year for Classification 4)
  2. Future Focus coordinators arrange services — personal care, cleaning, nursing, allied health, transport
  3. Trilogy claims the service cost from the government as the registered provider
  4. Trilogy pays Future Focus a coordination fee (your agreed %) on services coordinated
  5. Payment is automatic via RCTI (Recipient Created Tax Invoice) — monthly to Future Focus’s nominated account
Enterprise Advantage vs NDIS Hourly Billing

In NDIS, your revenue is capped by billable hours — one coordinator can only bill so many hours per week. In aged care, revenue scales with your client portfolio. More clients = more services coordinated = more fees. No cap, no rate freeze, no billable hours ceiling. And aged care clients require roughly half the coordination time of NDIS participants.

Team Sizing Note

In aged care, one coordinator can typically manage 25–35 clients (compared to 12–18 in NDIS). Future Focus can therefore serve more clients per coordinator, meaning your existing team has significant capacity to absorb aged care clients alongside their NDIS caseload — or dedicated coordinators can carry larger portfolios.

The Compounding Revenue Effect

Aged care clients stay an average of 2–3 years. Unlike NDIS where plans are reviewed annually and participants may change providers, aged care clients are significantly more stable. This means:

  • Year 1: You build your portfolio to 100+ clients. Revenue: $377K–$565K
  • Year 2: Most Year 1 clients remain. You add 100+ more. Revenue: $754K–$1.13M
  • Year 3: 150+ clients retained from Years 1–2. You add 100+ more. Revenue: $942K–$1.7M
Compounding vs Starting Over

In NDIS, annual plan reviews create churn risk. In aged care, your portfolio compounds. Every new client adds to a stable, recurring revenue base. By Year 3, you are earning from 3 years of accumulated clients, not just this year’s intake.

The Bottom Line for Future Focus

A Future Focus coordinator managing 30 aged care clients generates $141K in fee income at 12.5%, with roughly half the effort per client compared to NDIS. The same coordinator maxes out at ~$120K in NDIS with twice the admin. Aged care is higher revenue, lower effort, lower compliance, and the revenue compounds year over year.

How Payments Work — RCTI

RCTI = Recipient Created Tax Invoice. Instead of Future Focus invoicing Trilogy each month, Trilogy creates the invoice on your behalf and pays automatically. This eliminates all billing administration.

  • Payments: Monthly to Future Focus’s nominated bank account
  • Timeline: Paid within 5–7 business days of month end
  • Statement: Detailed breakdown by client, services coordinated, and fees earned — across all 9 locations
  • GST: Handled automatically
  • Reporting: Enterprise dashboard showing revenue by location, coordinator, and classification level
No Invoicing, No Chasing

In NDIS, you invoice plan managers and wait. With Trilogy, the payment process is fully automated across all 9 Future Focus locations. Your coordinators focus on care; the money arrives every month without a single invoice being raised.
