Referral Network Activation

12 min read — Multi-site referral strategy for Future Focus

The Enterprise Referral Advantage

Future Focus operates 9 locations across Victoria, Queensland, and Western Australia. Each location already maintains deep relationships with NDIS referral sources — allied health professionals, community organisations, and government agencies. This guide maps out how to activate aged care referral pathways at every location, leveraging your existing network to build a pipeline that compounds month over month.

9 Locations = 9 Referral Engines

Each Future Focus office has existing community relationships that translate directly to aged care referral sources. You are not starting from zero — you are activating what already exists.

Trilogy invests 50 foundation clients from day one, but the real growth engine is your local referral network. With 300+ partners already scaled into aged care, we know which referral channels deliver the highest volume and quality. This playbook adapts those learnings for a multi-site enterprise operation.

Hospital Discharge Teams — Highest-Volume Channel

Why hospitals matter most

Hospital discharge planners are responsible for ensuring patients have appropriate care in place before they leave. A single discharge planner can refer 5–10 aged care clients per month. Across 9 locations, if each office builds relationships with 2–3 hospitals, Future Focus could generate 90–270 referral opportunities per month from this channel alone.

How to identify the right contact at each hospital

  • Call the hospital switchboard and ask for the "Discharge planning team", "Patient flow coordinator", or "Social work department"
  • Some hospitals use "Transitional care" or "Hospital in the Home coordination"
  • Large hospitals have dedicated discharge planners specifically for aged care patients
  • Your NDIS coordinators likely already know social workers at these hospitals — ask them for warm introductions

First visit approach

  1. Leverage existing relationships: If your NDIS team already works with the hospital’s social work department, ask for an introduction to the aged care discharge team
  2. Call ahead to introduce yourself and ask when is a good time to visit
  3. Bring: Co-branded Future Focus × Trilogy Care brochures (10+), plus a quality morning tea
  4. Position the partnership: "We’re Future Focus, an NDIS coordination provider expanding into aged care through Trilogy Care — Australia’s 2nd largest Support at Home provider. We coordinate home care for patients transitioning from hospital, covering everything from personal care to nursing to allied health."
  5. Leave brochures and your coordinator’s direct contact card
  6. Ask: "What’s the best way for you to send us a referral?"
Enterprise Relationship Rules

Assign one coordinator per hospital — consistency builds trust faster than rotating contacts

Visit monthly — same day each month if possible

Close the loop on every referral: "Just wanted to let you know Mrs Smith is settled and her services are all set up. Thanks for the referral."

Be responsive — when they call, answer or call back within the hour

Report referral volume to Jake Kelly-Grubb monthly so Trilogy can track pipeline by location

GP Clinics — Steady, Reliable Pipeline

Your contact: the practice manager or practice nurse

GPs rarely refer directly. The practice manager or practice nurse handles referrals and recommends services to patients asking about home care. Many of your NDIS coordinators already have relationships with GP practices for NDIS plan reviews — these are warm introductions waiting to be activated.

Location-by-location activation

  1. Map GP practices within a 15km radius of each Future Focus office
  2. Prioritise: Practices with high elderly patient populations (look for bulk-billing clinics near retirement areas)
  3. Visit the practice and ask to speak with the practice manager
  4. Introduce the partnership: "We’re Future Focus, coordinating home care services for over-65s in [suburb] through Trilogy Care. Can I leave some information for your patients?"
  5. Leave 10 co-branded brochures at reception, plus a one-page flyer: "Are your patients getting the most from their Support at Home funding?"
  6. Follow up in 4 weeks, replenish materials, and ask about any patients who might benefit

Target: GP practices per location

Enterprise Target

Each Future Focus location should establish relationships with 5–10 GP practices within the first 90 days. That is 45–90 GP touchpoints across the network — a referral infrastructure no single-office partner can match.

Allied Health Networks — Leverage Your NDIS Relationships

This is where Future Focus has a massive advantage. Your coordinators already work with physios, OTs, speech pathologists, and psychologists across all 9 locations. Many of these allied health professionals see elderly clients alongside their NDIS caseload.

Why allied health referrals are high-quality

  • They visit clients at home — they see the reality of someone’s living situation
  • They build deep trust with patients over months or years
  • They naturally spot when someone needs more care support
  • Many already work across both NDIS and aged care — they understand the crossover

Retirement Villages & Lifestyle Communities

The opportunity across 3 states

Retirement villages (Ingenia, HomeTown, Aveo, Lendlease, local independents) are concentrated populations of the aged care demographic. With 9 offices across VIC, QLD, and WA, Future Focus can systematically build relationships with village managers in every region.

Approach — Village Manager

Call or visit the village office:

"I’m [Name] from Future Focus. We coordinate home care services for older Australians in the area through our partnership with Trilogy Care. Would it be possible to leave some information for your residents, or host a short information session about Support at Home funding?"

Most village managers welcome this — it adds value for their residents at no cost.

Hosting information sessions

  • Duration: 20–30 minutes + Q&A
  • Best time: Mid-morning (10am), mid-week
  • Topic: "Getting the Most from Your Support at Home Funding"
  • Bring: Co-branded brochures, a sign-up sheet for "free care needs assessment"
  • Follow up: Call everyone on the sign-up sheet within 3 business days
  • Scale: If a session works at one village, replicate the format across all 9 locations

Because aged care clients stay an average of 2–3 years, every new client adds to your recurring revenue base. At mature referral volumes, Future Focus could be adding 30–50 net new clients per month across the network — while retaining the hundreds already on the books.

Monthly review checklist (per location)
