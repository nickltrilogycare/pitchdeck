From 50 Foundation Clients to 250+

Trilogy invests 50 foundation clients into Future Focus from day one. That is not the ceiling — it is the launchpad. This playbook maps how your 9 locations across Victoria, Queensland, and Western Australia scale from that foundation to a portfolio of 250+ aged care clients within 12–18 months.

The growth model compounds. Every client your coordinators serve well generates referrals. Every hospital relationship activated generates a steady pipeline. Every NDIS family conversation opens a warm door into aged care.

The Growth Math

50 foundation clients at an average budget of $37,683/yr = $1.88M in Funds Under Management from day one.

250 clients at the same average = $9.42M FUM. At 10–15% coordination fee, that is $942K – $1.41M in annual coordination revenue for Future Focus.

Standard Trilogy partners start with zero clients and must build from scratch. Future Focus begins with 50 active clients generating revenue from week one. This is the core value of the enterprise tier — Trilogy invests in your launch so your team can focus on coordination and growth rather than cold-start acquisition.

Activate Your Existing NDIS Networks

Future Focus's most powerful growth lever is the network you already have. Your NDIS coordinators work with allied health professionals, support workers, and families every day. Many of those contacts have direct connections to people over 65 who need aged care.

NDIS client families

Your existing NDIS clients frequently have parents and grandparents over 65 who are either receiving home care or eligible to apply. This is the warmest referral pathway in aged care — your coordinators already have the relationship and the trust.

Across 9 locations with active NDIS caseloads, this pathway alone could generate 20–40 warm referrals in the first 3 months.

Allied health crossover

Physios, OTs, podiatrists, psychologists, and speech therapists in your NDIS network often work across both sectors. They see elderly patients every day. A single email or conversation from your coordinator opens a referral pathway that did not exist before.

Hospital Discharge Pipelines

Hospital discharge is the single highest-volume referral channel in aged care. Patients leaving hospital need home care arranged quickly, and discharge planners refer multiple clients per week. With 9 locations across 3 states, Future Focus can build relationships with 15–25 hospitals simultaneously.

How to Activate Each Hospital

Your coordinators should target the discharge planning team at every major hospital within 30 minutes of each Future Focus location.

  • Call the hospital switchboard and ask for the discharge planning or social work team
  • Introduce Future Focus as a local aged care coordinator partnered with Trilogy Care
  • Leave co-branded collateral with every discharge planner
  • Establish a monthly visit cadence — consistency converts to referrals
  • Position your coordinator as the reliable, responsive local option: "We can have someone introduced to the patient within 48 hours of referral"
Scale Advantage

With 9 locations, Future Focus can activate hospital relationships in parallel across VIC, QLD, and WA. A standard single-location partner might cover 2–3 hospitals. Your enterprise footprint allows 15–25 from launch.

3

GP and Allied Health Referral Networks

GPs see elderly patients weekly and are frequently asked about home care options. Allied health professionals who do home visits spot unmet care needs every day. Your coordinators build these relationships location by location.

  1. GP practices within 15 minutes of each location — Introduce to practice manager, leave co-branded brochures at reception, offer to be the named referral for home care coordination
  2. Home-visit allied health — Physios, OTs, and podiatrists who visit elderly clients at home are ideal referral partners. Cross-referral is a natural fit
  3. Community health centres — Often have social workers and aged care navigators who can refer directly
  4. Retirement villages and lifestyle communities — Village managers have direct access to residents who need home care. Offer information sessions for residents
  5. Local pharmacies — Pharmacists interact with elderly clients daily and are often trusted advisors on care needs
4

The Compounding Growth Model

Aged care client growth compounds. Unlike NDIS where plan reviews can disrupt continuity, aged care clients stay an average of 2–3 years. Every new client added to the portfolio remains in the portfolio, generating ongoing coordination revenue while your team continues adding new clients on top.

The Compounding Effect

"In month 1, you have 50 clients. In month 6, you have 100. But in month 12, you don't just have 150 — you have 150 plus every client from the previous 12 months who hasn't left. At 2–3 year average tenure, attrition is low and growth compounds on itself."

This is fundamentally different from project-based or plan-cycle work. Your aged care portfolio is an asset that grows in value every month.
