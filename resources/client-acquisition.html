Client Acquisition Playbook

15 min read

The 3-Step Framework

Most client growth comes from direct contact within your local community — people over 65 with Support at Home funding, or their family members. This playbook gives you a proven framework used by our most successful Coordination Partners.

The Framework at a Glance

Step 0: Tap your warm market (before any outbound)

Step 1: Co-branded brochure distribution

Step 2: Community outreach and relationship building

Step 3: Local events and public speaking

0

Your Warm Market Is Bigger Than You Think

Before you do any cold outreach, look at who you already know. Your existing network is your fastest path to your first clients.

Your NDIS clients' elderly parents

This is your #1 untapped opportunity. Your existing NDIS clients likely have parents or grandparents over 65 who either receive Support at Home services or are eligible to apply.

The conversation is natural: "I've started offering aged care coordination services. Would your mum or dad benefit from help managing their home care?"

Real Result

One coordinator told us: "I sent 15 messages to existing clients about their parents. Within a month I had 8 aged care referrals."

Your allied health network

Physios, OTs, psychologists, speech therapists, podiatrists — many work across both NDIS and aged care. They see elderly patients every day. One email introducing your new aged care service opens referral pathways.

Email Template

Subject: Quick heads up — I'm now offering aged care coordination

Hi [Name],

I wanted to let you know that I've expanded into aged care coordination through a partnership with Trilogy Care, one of Australia's largest Support at Home providers.

If you see any patients over 65 who might benefit from help managing their home care — or who are looking to switch providers for more care hours and less fees — I'd love a referral.

Happy to chat more about it over coffee. Would that be useful?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Business]
Coordination Partner — Trilogy Care

Your service provider contacts

Care workers, cleaners, nurses, and other providers in your NDIS network often work across both sectors. Ask: "Do you know anyone over 65 who's looking for a new home care coordinator?"

1

Co-Branded Brochure

Trilogy creates a professionally designed brochure with your business name and branding alongside Trilogy Care.

What the Brochure Includes
  • Your business name and logo
  • How the partnership works
  • What Support at Home is
  • How to get started
  • Contact details for both you and Trilogy

Who to give it to (prioritised)

  1. Hospital discharge planners — they refer daily
  2. GP reception desks — they see elderly patients weekly
  3. Allied health practices — physios, OTs, podiatrists
  4. Social workers — hospital and community
  5. Retirement village managers — direct access to residents
  6. RSL and community club notice boards — high foot traffic
  7. Community centres and seniors groups — engaged audiences
How to Get Yours

Submit your business details and logo to your Partnership Manager during Week 1 of onboarding. Brochure is typically ready within 5–7 business days.

2

Community Outreach

This is where you build the referral engine. The most successful Coordination Partners build ongoing relationships with local referral sources.

Hospital Discharge Units — Priority #1

Hospital discharge planners arrange home care for patients leaving hospital. They refer multiple clients per week.

How to approach:

  • Call the hospital and ask for the discharge planning team
  • Introduce yourself: "I'm [Name], a Support at Home care coordinator working with Trilogy Care. I'd love to leave some information about how we can support your patients transitioning home."
  • Bring: your co-branded brochure + a box of chocolates (seriously — this works)
  • Follow up: visit monthly. Consistency builds trust.
  • What to say: "I can make the transition home seamless for your patients. Here's how..."

GP Practices

GPs see elderly patients regularly and are often asked about home care options.

  • Introduce yourself to the practice manager (not the GP directly)
  • Leave brochures at reception
  • Offer to be a named referral for home care coordination
  • Follow up quarterly

Allied Health Home-Visit Practices

Physios, OTs, and podiatrists who do home visits are gold — they see clients at home and spot unmet care needs.

  • Send the email template above
  • Follow up with a phone call
  • Offer to cross-refer

Retirement Villages & Lifestyle Communities

Ingenia, HomeTown, and local independent living villages have residents who need home care.

  • Introduce yourself to the village manager
  • Ask about noticeboard opportunities
  • Offer to host an information talk for residents (see Step 3)

RSLs and Community Clubs

  • Speak to the sub-branch secretary or club manager
  • Ask about noticeboard placement
  • Sponsor a morning tea or social event

Community Centres and Seniors Groups

  • Contact the local council's seniors programs coordinator
  • Attend CALD community group meetings
  • Join carer support groups as a resource
3

Local Events

Public speaking and event presence builds credibility fast.

Retirement Village Talk (20-minute template)

Talk Structure
  1. Introduction — who you are, what care coordination means (3 min)
  2. What is Support at Home — the government program explained simply (5 min)
  3. How to get more from your funding — the Trilogy Care model (5 min)
  4. Q&A (5 min)
  5. Close — leave brochures, collect contact details (2 min)

Seniors Group Presentation

Similar to above but more informal. Focus on "Are you getting the most from your home care funding?" — a question that gets attention every time.

Business Networking (BNI, Chamber of Commerce)

Your 60-second elevator pitch:

Your 60-Second Pitch

"I'm [Name], and I coordinate home care services for older Australians through Trilogy Care, one of Australia's largest Support at Home providers. If you know anyone over 65 who needs help at home — or their family members — I'd love to have a conversation. I work locally in [area] and I'm always happy to do a free care needs chat."

Your First 3 Months — Activity Plan

Month 1

Foundation

  • Send warm market emails (NDIS clients, therapist network)
  • Get co-branded brochure from Trilogy
  • Visit 2 hospital discharge units
  • Drop brochures at 5 GP practices
Target: 3–5 conversations
Month 2

Momentum

  • Follow up on Month 1 contacts
  • Visit 2 more hospitals + 3 allied health practices
  • Introduce yourself to 2 retirement village managers
  • Host or attend 1 local event
Target: 5–8 active prospects
Month 3

Pipeline

  • Consistent follow-up cadence established
  • Monthly hospital visits locked in
  • 2+ referral sources actively sending leads
  • First 3–10 clients onboarded or in pipeline
Target: 8–15 prospects, 3–5 clients
