Enterprise Operating Model

10 min read — RACI framework for the Future Focus × Trilogy Care partnership

Partnership Structure

The enterprise partnership between Future Focus and Trilogy Care is designed for clarity at every level. Future Focus delivers care coordination through its existing team across 9 locations. Trilogy provides the registered provider infrastructure, technology platform, billing, compliance, and client pipeline. Neither party does the other’s job. This clear division is what makes the model scalable across multiple sites.

Enterprise Model Summary

Future Focus = coordinate care (what your team already does for NDIS, applied to aged care)
Trilogy Care = registration, billing, compliance, technology, referral pipeline, clinical oversight
The Client = participates in care planning, provides preferences, receives services

Future Focus Responsibilities

Your coordinators are the frontline of the client relationship. Everything they do in NDIS support coordination translates directly — the processes are simpler and the compliance burden is lighter.

Future Focus Coordinators

Care Coordination

  • Complete the Access Form and Care Plan preparation document with each client
  • Work with Trilogy to finalise care plans and budgets
  • Update care plans as client needs change over time
  • Be the primary day-to-day contact for clients and their families

Service Provider Management

  • Source and manage care workers and service providers (personal carers, cleaners, nurses, allied health)
  • Gather worker onboarding documents for Trilogy to verify
  • Create and publish weekly rosters, manage cancellations and substitutions
  • Update workers as client needs and preferences evolve

Client Relationship Management

  • Liaise with the client’s family (primarily adult children in aged care)
  • Log incidents in a timely manner through Trilogy’s platform
  • Record feedback and complaints for continuous improvement
  • Submit inclusions/exclusions requests with supporting documentation
  • Notify Trilogy if a client wishes to change or terminate services

Local Referral Network

  • Build and maintain referral relationships at each location (hospitals, GPs, allied health, retirement villages)
  • Report referral volumes and pipeline to Jake Kelly-Grubb monthly
  • Leverage existing NDIS relationships for warm introductions to aged care referral sources

Trilogy Care Responsibilities

Trilogy handles everything behind the scenes so Future Focus coordinators can focus entirely on client relationships and care delivery.

Trilogy Care — Provider Infrastructure

Registration & Compliance

  • Hold and maintain the aged care provider registration — Future Focus does not need its own
  • Manage all government reporting, audits, and regulatory compliance
  • Annual compliance is a 45-minute phone call, not an 18-month NDIS audit cycle
  • Clinical incident management and risk adjustment
  • Continuous improvement processes from client feedback

Technology Platform

  • Provide the Trilogy Care portal for care plan management, rostering, and incident logging
  • Enterprise dashboard for Future Focus leadership to view performance across all 9 locations
  • Onboarding training for all Future Focus coordinators transitioning to the platform

Financial Management & Billing

  • Claim government funding on behalf of all clients
  • Manage expenditure tracking against each client’s budget
  • Approve/reject invoices and bills from service providers
  • Publish monthly client statements
  • Collect Income Tested Fees where applicable
  • Pay Future Focus coordination fees automatically via RCTI — no invoicing required

Workforce Compliance

  • Verify and store all mandatory worker documents (police checks, insurance, vaccinations)
  • Future Focus coordinators gather documents; Trilogy verifies and maintains the compliance register

Referral Pipeline & Growth

  • Invest 50 foundation clients from day one of the partnership
  • Provide co-branded marketing materials for all 9 Future Focus locations
  • National marketing campaigns that drive referrals to local partner coordinators
  • Ongoing pipeline support through Trilogy’s referral network

Client Responsibilities

What the Client Does

  • Participate in care plan conversations and reviews
  • Provide roster preferences and feedback on care workers
  • Inform their coordinator or Trilogy of any incidents
  • Submit reimbursement requests for approved out-of-pocket expenses
  • Pay Income Tested Fee if applicable (assessed by Services Australia, not by Trilogy or Future Focus)
  • Attend scheduled care plan reviews

Governance & Review Cadence

Enterprise partnerships require structured governance to ensure both parties are aligned, performance is tracked, and issues are resolved promptly.

Escalation Paths

Clear escalation paths ensure nothing gets stuck. Most issues resolve at Level 1. If they do not, the escalation is fast and direct.

Level Issue Type FF Contact Trilogy Contact Response Time
1 Day-to-day operational (rostering, care plans, platform) FF Coordinator Jake Kelly-Grubb Same business day
2 Clinical incident or compliance concern FF operations lead Trilogy clinical team (via Jake) Within 4 hours
3 Financial dispute or billing issue FF leadership Nick Lunn Within 2 business days
4 Strategic/contractual (partnership terms, expansion) FF executive Luke Traini Within 5 business days
Multi-Site Operations

Because Future Focus operates across 9 locations in 3 states, Jake Kelly-Grubb serves as the single coordination point for all sites. Future Focus leadership maintains oversight of their coordinators, while Jake ensures consistency of service delivery standards, reporting, and compliance across the entire network.
