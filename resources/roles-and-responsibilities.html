Your Role as Care Coordinator

Your core job: coordinate care services for your clients according to their approved care plan and budget. Below is the full breakdown of what that looks like day-to-day.

Care Coordinator Responsibilities

Care Planning

  • Complete the Access Form and Care Plan preparation document with the client
  • Work with Trilogy to finalise the care plan
  • Update the care plan as client needs change

Worker Sourcing & Management

  • Source workers and service providers (personal carers, cleaners, nurses, allied health)
  • Assist in gathering worker onboarding documents
  • Roster employees, publish rosters weekly, manage cancellations
  • Update workers as client needs and preferences change

Client Management

  • Be the primary contact for day-to-day care coordination
  • Liaise with the client’s family
  • Log incidents in a timely fashion
  • Log feedback and complaints with Trilogy Care
  • Submit inclusions/exclusions requests with supporting documents

Administrative Support

  • Assist Trilogy with financial queries where possible
  • Request package upgrades (with supporting information)
  • Log meal requests, taxi/cab charges, OT assessments with Trilogy
  • Notify Trilogy if a client wishes to terminate

Trilogy Care’s Role as Provider

Trilogy handles everything behind the scenes so you can focus on care coordination.

Trilogy Care Responsibilities

Care Plan & Compliance

  • Finalise care plans and budgets with you and the client
  • Provide printed copy of care plan for client’s home
  • Conduct care plan reviews
  • Manage clinical incidents, adjust risks as required
  • Continuous improvement from feedback

Workforce Compliance

  • Mandatory document checking
  • COVID vaccination verification
  • Police checks
  • Insurance verification
  • All gathered by you, verified and stored by Trilogy

Financial Management

  • Manage expenditure in line with budget
  • Approve/reject bills and invoices
  • Publish monthly statements
  • Collect Income Tested Fees
  • Process reimbursements

Other

  • Approve/reject inclusions/exclusions requests
  • Submit meal and transport requests
  • Process package upgrades and update care plans

Client Responsibilities

What Your Client Is Responsible For

  • Participate in care plan conversations
  • Provide roster preferences and feedback
  • Inform coordinator/Trilogy of incidents
  • Submit reimbursement requests
  • Pay Income Tested Fee (if applicable)
  • Attend care plan reviews
Key Takeaway

The three-way partnership is simple: You coordinate, Trilogy administers, the client participates. Each party has clear responsibilities, reducing confusion and ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

NDIS vs Aged Care: What’s the Same, What’s Different

What stays the same

If you’re coming from an NDIS coordination background, many of your core skills transfer directly.

Activity NDIS Aged Care
Coordinate care services ✓ ✓
Manage budgets ✓ ✓
Liaise with providers ✓ ✓
Advocate for clients ✓ ✓
Log incidents/complaints ✓ ✓
Source workers ✓ ✓
Work independently ✓ ✓

What’s different

While the core coordination skills transfer, there are meaningful structural differences that generally work in your favour.

Factor NDIS Aged Care
Complexity High — behaviour support, SIL, restrictive practices Lower — connection service, no restrictive practices
Compliance burden 18-month audit cycle, on-site, hundreds of hours prep 45-minute annual phone call
Billing You invoice, chase plan managers Trilogy handles all billing, you receive RCTI
Registration You may need your own Trilogy carries the registration
Client relationship Directly with participant Primarily with family (adult children)
Funding mechanism NDIA plan, annual review SAH quarterly budget, no reviews
Clinical oversight Arrange externally Phone access to Trilogy nursing team
Bottom Line

Aged care coordination through Trilogy is less complex, less admin, less compliance — and you get paid automatically. The skills you already have translate directly; the friction you’re used to largely disappears.
