Support at Home — What Your Coordinators Need to Know

14 min read

Aged Care Funding for NDIS Professionals

Your Future Focus coordinators already understand person-centred planning, budget management, and service coordination. Support at Home (SaH) applies those same skills to a different funding stream — one that serves older Australians rather than people with disability.

This resource translates the aged care system into language your team already speaks. Everything here maps directly to NDIS concepts your coordinators work with every day.

The Core Parallel

Think of Support at Home as the aged care equivalent of an NDIS plan. It is a government-funded individual budget for support services. The key differences: there are no plan reviews, funding is released quarterly (not as a lump sum), and the budget is determined by classification level rather than individual planning meetings. The skills transfer is direct — your coordinators already know how to do this work.

What Is Support at Home?

Support at Home (SaH) is the Australian Government's primary home-based aged care programme. It launched on 1 July 2025, replacing the former Home Care Package (HCP) system. The programme funds care services for older Australians so they can live independently at home for as long as possible.

Each client receives an individual budget based on their assessed needs. That budget is managed by a registered provider (Trilogy Care) and the services within it are coordinated by people like your Future Focus team.

Who Qualifies?

  • Australians aged 65 and over (or 50+ for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people)
  • People who need ongoing support to live independently at home
  • Must be assessed through My Aged Care — the aged care equivalent of the NDIA
For Your Coordinators

Many of your existing NDIS clients have parents or grandparents who are eligible for SaH. This is one of the most natural referral pathways your team will encounter — a conversation they can have in every client interaction.

The My Aged Care Process

This is the client's journey before they reach your coordinators. Understanding it helps your team have informed conversations with new clients and referral sources.

  1. Contact My Aged Care — The client (or their family) calls 1800 200 422 or visits myagedcare.gov.au to register
  2. Complete an assessment — A trained assessor evaluates their care needs in their home. This is similar to an NDIS functional assessment but focused on ageing-related needs
  3. Receive a classification — The client is assigned one of 8 classification levels, each with a defined annual budget
  4. Choose a provider — This is where Future Focus and Trilogy Care enter. The client selects Trilogy as their approved provider, and your coordinator is assigned
  5. Start receiving services — Your coordinator builds the care plan, sources providers, and manages ongoing service delivery

What Services Are Funded

Your coordinators will recognise many of these service categories from NDIS work. Services must align with the client's assessed needs and agreed care plan:

  • Personal care — bathing, grooming, dressing, toileting (similar to NDIS core supports)
  • Nursing and allied health — physio, podiatry, OT, speech therapy, dietetics
  • Meal preparation and delivery
  • Domestic assistance — cleaning, laundry, garden maintenance
  • Home modifications and assistive technology — ramps, rails, mobility aids
  • Transport — to appointments and social activities
  • Social support — outings, group programs, companionship visits
  • Care coordination — the service your coordinators deliver

Key Differences From NDIS Funding

  • No plan reviews or reassessment cycles — budgets roll quarterly
  • Lower complexity overall — no behaviour support plans, SIL, or SDA equivalents
  • Funding is released quarterly rather than as a lump sum
  • Care plans are collaborative but simpler than NDIS plans
  • Clients are generally more stable and require less crisis management

Care Planning: NDIS vs Aged Care

What Your Team Knows (NDIS)

  • Goals-based planning with detailed NDIS plan structure
  • Service agreements with individual providers
  • Plan reviews every 12–24 months
  • Complex stakeholder management (LACs, planners, support workers)
  • Incident reporting and NDIS Quality and Safeguards
  • Budget tracking across multiple categories

What Transfers to Aged Care

  • Person-centred care plans focused on independence and wellbeing
  • Service provider sourcing and coordination
  • Ongoing quarterly budget management (no plan reviews)
  • Simpler stakeholder set (client, family, GP, Trilogy)
  • Incident reporting through Trilogy's portal
  • Single budget pool — more flexibility in how funds are allocated

Additional Funding Streams

Beyond the core classification budget, clients may be eligible for supplementary funding. These are separate allocations that your coordinators can access:

$15,000
Assistive Technology
Lifetime budget for mobility aids, personal alarms, and other assistive devices. Similar to NDIS AT funding.
$15,000
Home Modifications
Separate lifetime budget for ramps, rails, bathroom modifications, and structural changes to the home.
$12,000
Restorative Care
Up to 16 weeks of short-term, goal-focused recovery support. Similar to NDIS capacity building.
End-of-Life Pathway

Clients may also access up to $25,000 over 16 weeks for end-of-life care focused on comfort and dignity. This is assessed and approved separately through Trilogy's clinical team.

Client Contributions (Means-Tested Fees)

Some clients pay an Income Tested Fee (ITF) based on their financial situation. This is assessed by Services Australia (Centrelink), not by your coordinators or Trilogy. Your team does not need to calculate or manage this.

What Your Coordinators Need to Know
  • Full pensioners generally pay little or no ITF
  • Self-funded retirees and part-pensioners may contribute more
  • The fee is capped and cannot exceed a set percentage of the budget
  • Trilogy and Services Australia handle all ITF calculations and collection
  • Your coordinators never need to discuss fees with clients — Trilogy manages this
