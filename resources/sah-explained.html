Support at Home Explained

12 min read

What Is Support at Home?

Support at Home (SAH) is a government-funded program that helps older Australians live safely and independently in their own home. It launched on 1 November 2025, replacing the Home Care Package (HCP) system.

The program provides funding for care services based on a person's assessed needs. Each client receives an individual budget, managed by a registered provider (like Trilogy Care), and used to pay for services coordinated by people like you.

Coming from NDIS?

Think of SAH as the aged care equivalent of an NDIS plan. It's a government-funded individual budget for support services. The key differences: there are no plan reviews, funding is released quarterly (not as a lump sum), and the budget is capped by classification level.

Who Qualifies?

  • Australians aged 65 and over (or 50+ for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people)
  • People who need help to live independently at home
  • Must be assessed through My Aged Care (the aged care equivalent of the NDIA)

How a Client Applies

  1. Contact My Aged Care — Call 1800 200 422 or visit myagedcare.gov.au
  2. Complete an assessment — A trained assessor evaluates their care needs
  3. Receive a classification — Assigned one of 8 levels based on need and budget
  4. Choose a provider — This is where you and Trilogy Care come in
  5. Start receiving services — You coordinate, Trilogy administers

What's Funded

Services must be based on assessed care needs, agreed care plan, and individual budget:

  • Personal care (bathing, grooming, dressing)
  • Nursing and allied health (physio, podiatry, OT)
  • Meal preparation and delivery
  • Cleaning, laundry, garden maintenance
  • Home modifications and assistive technology
  • Transport
  • Social outings, groups, and visitors
  • Care management and coordination

What's NOT Funded

  • Household bills and food purchases
  • Insurance
  • Home appliances unrelated to ageing
  • Accommodation costs
  • Services funded under other government programs (medications, dental, ambulance, medical gap payments)

Additional Funding Streams

In addition to the core budget, clients may be eligible for:

Assistive Technology

$15,000

Separate lifetime budget for ramps, rails, bathroom modifications, and structural changes.
$12,000
Restorative Care
Up to 16 weeks of short-term, goal-focused recovery support.
End-of-Life Pathway

Clients may also access up to $25,000 over 16 weeks for end-of-life care focused on comfort and dignity. This is assessed and approved separately.

Client Contributions (Means-Tested Fees)

Some clients pay an Income Tested Fee (ITF) based on their financial situation. This is assessed by Services Australia (Centrelink), not by you or Trilogy. The ITF reduces the government subsidy, not the client's care plan.

Key Points
  • Full pensioners generally pay little or no ITF
  • Self-funded retirees and part-pensioners may pay more
  • The fee is capped and can't exceed a certain percentage of the budget
  • You don't need to calculate this — Trilogy and Services Australia handle it
